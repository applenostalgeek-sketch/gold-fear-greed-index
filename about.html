What is Fear & Greed?

Markets are driven by two fundamental emotions: fear and greed. When fear dominates, investors sell aggressively, often creating oversold conditions and buying opportunities. When greed takes over, markets become euphoric and overbought, increasing the risk of corrections.

Our indices quantify this psychology across four asset classes‚ÄîGold (safe haven), Bonds (risk-off), Stocks (growth), and Crypto (speculation)‚Äîby analyzing multiple technical and sentiment indicators. Each index scores from 0 to 100:

  • 0-25 (Extreme Fear): Panic selling, potential bottoms
  • 25-45 (Fear): Cautious sentiment, defensive positioning
  • 45-55 (Neutral): Balanced market, no extreme bias
  • 55-75 (Greed): Optimistic sentiment, risk appetite rising
  • 75-100 (Extreme Greed): Euphoria, FOMO, potential tops

Key insight: Extreme readings are contrarian signals. When everyone is fearful, it's often the best time to buy. When everyone is greedy, caution is warranted.

ü™ô Gold Fear & Greed Index

The Gold Index measures sentiment TOWARDS gold using 5 components. Philosophy: Like Alternative.me measures crypto sentiment, we measure whether investors are buying or selling gold, regardless of the reason. All scores from 0-100:

1. GLD Price Momentum (30%)

Direct 14-day GLD performance (PRIMARY INDICATOR)

Highest weight component. Measures direct SPDR Gold Trust (GLD) price change over 14 days. GLD +7% = score 100 (extreme greed), GLD -7% = score 0 (extreme fear). This captures actual buying/selling sentiment regardless of macro reasons. Uses a √ó7 multiplier centered at 50.

2. RSI & Moving Averages (25%)

Mean-reversion and trend signal

Combines 14-day RSI with price position relative to 50-day and 200-day moving averages. Uses proportional distance-based scoring: contribution scales with how far price is above/below each MA, rather than binary on/off signals. RSI provides a ¬±25 point contribution centered at 50. Price above MA50 by 10% = strong bullish signal, price below both MAs with RSI < 30 = extreme fear.

3. Dollar Index (20%)

USD strength (inverted correlation)

Gold and the dollar typically move inversely. Weakening dollar = stronger gold demand = higher score. Tracks 14-day change in DXY (US Dollar Index vs basket of currencies) with a √ó15 multiplier. Dollar down 3.3% = score 100, dollar up 3.3% = score 0. Also factors in DXY position relative to its 30-day moving average.

4. Real Rates (15%)

10-Year TIPS yield (inflation-adjusted)

Gold is a non-yielding asset, so lower real rates = more attractive gold = higher score. Uses FRED API (DFII10 - 10-Year Treasury Inflation-Indexed Security) with Yahoo fallback (TIP ETF). Real rate at -1% = score ~94, at +3% = score ~19. Fundamental driver of gold valuation.

5. VIX (10%)

Safe haven demand (z-score normalized)

Uses a z-score approach: compares current VIX to its 3-month average and standard deviation. VIX significantly above average = market stress = increased safe haven gold demand = higher score. Score = 50 + (z-score √ó 25), capped 0-100. This contextualizes the VIX level rather than using fixed thresholds, making the signal adaptive to changing volatility regimes.

üìä Bonds Fear & Greed Index

The Bonds Index measures sentiment in the fixed income market using 6 bond-specific components with zero overlap with equity markets. Optimized to measure bond demand, duration appetite, and credit conditions. All scores from 0-100:

1. Yield Curve Shape (20%)

10-Year vs 2-Year Treasury spread (FRED API with Yahoo fallback)

Classic recession indicator measuring yield curve shape. Inverted logic (sentiment perspective): Steep curve (+2%+) = strong economy = investors favor STOCKS over bonds = low bonds score. Flat/Normal curve (0% to +1%) = moderate bonds score. Inverted curve (< 0%) = recession fear = flight to bonds = high score (up to 100). Uses FRED API data (DGS2, DGS10) with Yahoo fallback (^TNX, ^IRX) when FRED unavailable.

2. Duration Risk / TLT (20%)

TLT 14-day price momentum

Measures demand for long-duration Treasuries via iShares 20+ Year Treasury ETF (TLT). Rising TLT = investors buying bonds = high score. Falling TLT = investors selling = low score. Uses √ó12.5 multiplier: TLT +4% = score 100, TLT -4% = score 0. Captures real-time bond appetite.

3. Credit Quality Appetite (20%)

Investment Grade (LQD) vs Treasuries (TLT)

Measures demand for investment-grade corporate bonds relative to safe Treasuries. LQD outperforming TLT = credit spreads tightening = high score (investors taking moderate credit risk). LQD underperforming = credit stress = low score. Uses iShares iBoxx Investment Grade Corporate Bond ETF (LQD). Note: Different from junk bonds (HYG) used in Stocks index‚Äîthis measures quality corporate credit, not high-risk debt.

4. Real Rates Attractiveness (15%)

10-Year TIPS yield (inflation-adjusted returns)

Real yield = nominal yield minus inflation expectations. Positive real yields = bonds paying above inflation = attractive investment = higher score. Negative real yields = inflation destroying returns = unattractive = low score. Uses FRED API (DFII10) with Yahoo fallback. Score = 50 + (real_rate √ó 6), providing moderate sensitivity: +2% real = 62, 0% = 50, -2% = 38.

5. Bond Volatility (15%)

Short-term vs long-term TLT volatility (MOVE proxy)

Compares 5-day TLT annualized volatility to 30-day average as a proxy for the MOVE index (bond market VIX). Rising short-term volatility = bond market stress = fear = lower score. Calm, stable bond markets = confidence = higher score. Uses √ó75 multiplier for sensitivity to sudden volatility spikes. This component detected the April 2025 basis trade crisis and May 2025 Moody's downgrade stress.

6. Equity vs Bonds Rotation (10%)

TLT vs SPY relative performance

Measures relative performance between stocks and bonds over 14 days. Bonds outperforming stocks = investors favoring bonds = high score (greed for bonds). Stocks outperforming = investors leaving bonds = low score (fear for bonds). Uses √ó8 multiplier. Independent signal capturing broader risk-on/risk-off rotation.

üîÑ Data Reliability: Yahoo Finance Fallbacks

Weekend/Holiday Protection: When FRED API is unavailable (weekends, holidays, outages), the Bonds index automatically falls back to Yahoo Finance for Yield Curve (^TNX, ^IRX) and Real Rates (TIP ETF). This ensures 65% of the index (Duration, Credit Quality, Bond Volatility, Equity vs Bonds) continues working normally as these components only use Yahoo data. The remaining 35% (Yield Curve + Real Rates) uses FRED‚ÜíYahoo fallback hierarchy, never defaulting to stale 50.0 neutral values.

üìà Stocks Fear & Greed Index

The Stocks Index measures sentiment towards equities using 7 components. Philosophy: combines direct price performance with volatility, breadth, rotation, and cross-asset signals to capture when "people are buying stocks" or "people are panicking and selling". All scores from 0-100:

1. Price Strength (20%)

Direct SPY 14-day performance

Measures S&P 500 (SPY) price change over 14 days with a √ó5 multiplier. SPY +10% = people buying stocks = high score. SPY -10% = people selling = low score. This directly captures buying/selling sentiment. When stocks crash -15%, this component drives the score to Extreme Fear, signaling panic selling.

2. VIX (20%)

Market fear gauge (CBOE Volatility Index)

Measures expected stock market volatility using a continuous linear formula: score = 90 - (VIX - 10) √ó 3.2, capped 0-100. VIX 10 = score 90 (extreme complacency). VIX 20 = score 58. VIX 30 = score 26. VIX 38+ = score 0. Smooth transitions eliminate artificial cliff effects between VIX thresholds.

3. Momentum (15%)

RSI + 50-day moving average position

Combines 14-day RSI (60% weight) with price position relative to 50-day MA (40% weight). RSI >70 + Price > MA50 = overbought/bullish = high score. RSI <30 + Price < MA50 = oversold/bearish = low score. Captures technical momentum and trend strength beyond raw price moves.

4. Market Participation (15%)

Equal-weight vs cap-weight performance (RSP vs SPY)

Compares Invesco S&P 500 Equal Weight ETF (RSP) to SPY over 14 days with a √ó12 multiplier. RSP outperforming SPY = broad participation across all stocks = healthy greed. SPY outperforming RSP = narrow leadership (only mega-caps rising) = fragile market = lower score. Saturates at ¬±4.2% divergence.

5. Junk Bond Demand (10%)

High-yield corporate bonds vs Treasuries (HYG vs TLT)

Compares iShares High Yield Corporate Bond ETF (HYG) to 20+ Year Treasury ETF (TLT) over 14 days. HYG outperforming TLT = risk appetite for junk bonds = greed. TLT outperforming HYG = flight to safety from risky corporate debt = fear. Credit markets often signal equity sentiment changes before they appear in stock prices.

6. Safe Haven Demand (10%)

TLT 14-day momentum (inverted)

Measures flight-to-safety flows via iShares 20+ Year Treasury ETF (TLT). Rising TLT = investors buying safe Treasuries = fear for stocks = lower score. Falling TLT = investors leaving safe havens = confidence in stocks = higher score. Independent signal that captures defensive positioning before it shows in stock prices.

7. Sector Rotation (10%)

Tech vs Defensive rotation (QQQ vs XLP)

Measures sentiment shift between offensive and defensive sectors via 14-day relative performance with a √ó3 multiplier. QQQ (Nasdaq-100 tech) outperforming XLP (Consumer Staples) = risk-on = greed. XLP outperforming QQQ = defensive rotation = fear. Saturates at ¬±16.7% divergence.

‚Çø Crypto Fear & Greed Index

The Crypto Index measures sentiment towards crypto using 5 components. Philosophy: Calibrated against Alternative.me's methodology. Focuses on behavioral indicators, volume dynamics, and volatility rather than raw price alone. All scores from 0-100:

1. Context (30%)

30-day BTC price trend (PRIMARY INDICATOR)

Highest weight component. Captures the medium-term trend that drives overall sentiment. Score = 50 + (30-day change √ó 2.0), capped 0-100. Bitcoin +25% over 30 days = score 100 (extreme greed). Bitcoin -25% = score 0 (extreme fear). Centered at 50 (neutral) with full range 0-100. This reflects the reality that prolonged price movement‚Äînot single-day spikes‚Äîshapes investor psychology.

2. RSI & Moving Averages (20%)

Bitcoin technical momentum

Combines RSI directly as a 0-100 score (60% weight) with MA position (40% weight). RSI below 30 = extreme fear, RSI above 70 = extreme greed. Moving average position provides trend context: price above both MA50 and MA200 signals bull market confidence, while price below both MAs indicates bear market fear.

3. Bitcoin Dominance (20%)

BTC vs ETH performance (inverted)

Tracks relative performance between Bitcoin (digital gold) and Ethereum (altcoin proxy) with a √ó3.0 multiplier centered at 50. INVERTED logic: When Bitcoin outperforms Ethereum, investors are rotating TO safety (BTC) FROM risk (alts) = fear = lower score. When Ethereum outperforms Bitcoin, "altcoin season" = risk appetite = greed = higher score. Example: BTC +10% while ETH +20% over 14 days = ETH outperforms by 10pp = greed signal.

4. Volume Trend (15%)

7-day vs 30-day average volume, direction-adjusted

Price-independent signal measuring trading activity dynamics. Compares 7-day average volume to 30-day average, then adjusts for price direction with a √ó50 multiplier. High volume + rising price = greed (buying pressure). High volume + falling price = fear (panic selling). Low volume = neutral. This captures market participation intensity beyond just price movement.

5. Volatility (15%)

14-day annualized volatility (inverted)

Crypto's wild price swings reveal market psychology with thresholds calibrated for crypto: Volatility ≥ 80% annualized = score 0 (extreme fear). Volatility ≤ 40% = score 100 (calm confidence). Between 40-80%: linear interpolation. These thresholds reflect crypto's naturally higher volatility compared to traditional assets. When Bitcoin swings ±10% daily, investors are nervous.

Market Sentiment Indicator (Fear vs Greed)

Measures overall market mood by comparing how investors feel about risk assets (Stocks + Crypto) versus safe havens (Bonds + Gold). When money flows toward safety, the market is fearful. When money flows toward risk, the market is greedy.

Calculation

Greed Side = (Stocks Score + Crypto Score) / 2
Fear Side = (Bonds Score + Gold Score) / 2
Market Sentiment = Greed Side - Fear Side
Position = ((Market Sentiment + 100) / 200) √ó 100

This produces a 0-100 score. Below 50, fear dominates ‚Äî investors prefer safe havens. Above 50, greed dominates ‚Äî investors chase risk. The calculation uses equal weighting (50/50) within each side to avoid bias toward traditional or alternative markets.

Interpretation

  • 0-25 (Extreme Fear): Investors fleeing to safe havens (bonds + gold), markets in distress
  • 25-45 (Fear): Cautious sentiment, investors favoring safety over risk
  • 45-55 (Neutral): No clear direction, balanced between fear and greed
  • 55-75 (Greed): Growing risk appetite, investors favoring stocks and crypto
  • 75-100 (Extreme Greed): Euphoric risk-taking across assets, potential top warning

Why this approach? Traditional markets (stocks/bonds) and alternative assets (crypto/gold) each reveal different aspects of investor psychology. By combining all four indices, we capture a more complete picture of market mood than any single asset could provide.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the Fear and Greed Index?

Our indices use multi-component quantitative models designed to capture market sentiment shifts. Extreme readings (< 25 or > 75) tend to be more reliable as contrarian signals, while mid-range values reflect genuine market uncertainty. These are analytical tools designed to complement‚Äînot replace‚Äîfundamental analysis and risk management. No sentiment indicator is perfectly predictive, and past patterns may not repeat in future market conditions.

Why does the index sometimes react slowly to major news?

Most components use 14-day windows to capture sustained sentiment shifts rather than panic reactions. Some faster signals exist (VIX z-score, RSI, crypto volume 7-day), but the overall index typically takes 2-4 days to fully reflect sudden events. This is intentional‚Äîwe track structural sentiment trends, not headlines. For day trading signals, other tools may be more appropriate.

CNN's index only covers US stocks. We provide four separate indices for Bonds, Gold, Stocks, and Crypto, enabling comprehensive cross-asset sentiment analysis. Why are there different numbers of components for each index?

Each asset class has unique market dynamics requiring tailored indicators:

  • Gold (5 components): Safe haven behavior tracked via GLD price momentum (30%), RSI/MA mean-reversion (25%), dollar strength (20%), real rates (15%), and VIX z-score (10%). Captures flight-to-safety dynamics with diversified signals.
  • Bonds (6 components): Bond market sentiment focuses purely on fixed income indicators‚Äîyield curve shape (20%), TLT demand (20%), credit quality spreads (20%), real yields (15%), bond volatility (15%), and equity vs bonds rotation (10%). Zero overlap with equity markets ensures independent measurement.
  • Stocks (7 components): Equity sentiment captures price strength (20%), VIX (20%), RSI momentum (15%), market participation (15%), junk bond demand (10%), safe haven demand (10%), and sector rotation (10%). Multi-faceted approach with both technical and cross-asset signals.
  • Crypto (5 components): 30-day trend context (30%), RSI/MA momentum (20%), Bitcoin dominance vs ETH (20%), volume trend (15%), and volatility (15%). Calibrated for crypto's unique volatility profile with direction-adjusted volume as a price-independent signal.

More components doesn't mean better‚Äîit means more relevant. Each index is optimized for its specific market.
