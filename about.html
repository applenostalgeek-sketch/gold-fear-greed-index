What is Fear & Greed?

Markets are driven by two fundamental emotions: fear and greed. When fear dominates, investors sell aggressively, often creating oversold conditions and buying opportunities. When greed takes over, markets become euphoric and overbought, increasing the risk of corrections.

Our indices quantify this psychology across three asset classes‚ÄîGold (safe haven), Stocks (growth), and Crypto (speculation)‚Äîby analyzing multiple technical and sentiment indicators. Each index scores from 0 to 100:

  • 0-25 (Extreme Fear): Panic selling, potential bottoms
  • 25-45 (Fear): Cautious sentiment, defensive positioning
  • 45-55 (Neutral): Balanced market, no extreme bias
  • 55-75 (Greed): Optimistic sentiment, risk appetite rising
  • 75-100 (Extreme Greed): Euphoria, FOMO, potential tops

Key insight: Extreme readings are contrarian signals. When everyone is fearful, it's often the best time to buy. When everyone is greedy, caution is warranted.

ü™ô Gold Fear & Greed Index

The Gold Index measures sentiment in the gold market using 7 components with varying weights. All scores from 0-100:

1. Volatility (15%)

14-day volatility vs 30-day average

Lower volatility indicates confidence (greed), higher volatility suggests uncertainty (fear). Calculated from daily returns of gold futures (GC=F).

2. Momentum (25%)

RSI + 50-day & 200-day moving averages

Combines 14-day RSI with price position relative to 50-day and 200-day MAs. Price above both MAs = strong bullish momentum. RSI >70 = overbought, <30 = oversold.

3. Gold vs S&P 500 (15%)

14-day relative performance

Gold outperforming stocks = flight to safety. Stocks outperforming gold = risk-on sentiment.

4. ETF Flows (15%)

GLD volume and price trends

Analyzes SPDR Gold Trust (GLD) trading volume and price as proxy for institutional flows.

5. VIX (10%)

Market fear gauge

High VIX = market stress = increased gold demand. Low VIX = complacency.

6. Real Rates (10%)

10-Year TIPS yield

Lower real rates make non-yielding gold more attractive. Uses FRED API (DFII10) with Yahoo fallback (TIP ETF).

7. Dollar Index (10%)

USD strength (inverted)

Gold typically moves inversely to the dollar. Weakening dollar = stronger gold.

üìä Bonds Fear & Greed Index

The Bonds Index measures sentiment in the fixed income market using 5 bond-specific components with zero overlap with equity markets. Optimized to measure capital flows and duration demand. All scores from 0-100:

1. Duration Risk / Capital Flow (30%)

TLT 14-day price momentum (highest weight)

Measures direct capital flows into long-duration Treasuries via iShares 20+ Year Treasury ETF (TLT). Rising TLT = capital flowing INTO bonds = high score. Falling TLT = capital leaving = low score. Uses aggressive scoring: TLT +3% = score 80, TLT -3% = score 20. This is the primary indicator of bond market sentiment, capturing real-time capital rotation.

2. Term Premium Demand (25%)

Long-term (TLT) vs Short-term (SHY) relative performance

Compares 20+ Year Treasuries (TLT) vs 1-3 Year Treasuries (SHY). TLT outperforming = investors seeking duration exposure = high score (demand for term premium). SHY outperforming = rate risk aversion = low score (staying short-duration). Measures whether investors are willing to take duration risk for higher yields.

3. Credit Quality Appetite (20%)

Investment Grade (LQD) vs Treasuries (TLT)

Measures demand for investment-grade corporate bonds relative to safe Treasuries. LQD outperforming TLT = credit spreads tightening = high score (investors taking moderate credit risk). LQD underperforming = credit stress = low score. Uses iShares iBoxx Investment Grade Corporate Bond ETF (LQD). Note: Different from junk bonds (HYG) used in Stocks index‚Äîthis measures quality corporate credit, not high-risk debt.

4. Yield Curve Shape (15%)

10-Year vs 2-Year Treasury spread (FRED API with Yahoo fallback)

Classic recession indicator measuring yield curve shape. Inverted logic (capital rotation perspective): Steep curve (+2%+) = strong economy = capital flows to STOCKS (not bonds) = low bonds score. Flat/Normal curve (0% to +1%) = moderate bonds score. Inverted curve (< 0%) = recession fear = flight to bonds = high score (up to 100). Uses FRED API data (DGS2, DGS10) with Yahoo fallback (^TNX, ^IRX) when FRED unavailable.

5. Real Rates Attractiveness (10%)

10-Year TIPS yield (inflation-adjusted returns)

Real yield = nominal yield minus inflation expectations. Positive real yields = bonds paying above inflation = attractive investment = higher score. Negative real yields = inflation destroying returns = unattractive = low score. Uses FRED API (DFII10) with Yahoo fallback (TIP ETF + ^TNX estimation). Conservative scoring: +4% real = 82, 0% = 50, -4% = 18. Real yields > +1.5% indicate very attractive bond valuations historically.

üîÑ Data Reliability: Yahoo Finance Fallbacks

Weekend/Holiday Protection: When FRED API is unavailable (weekends, holidays, outages), the Bonds index automatically falls back to Yahoo Finance for Yield Curve (^TNX, ^IRX) and Real Rates (TIP ETF). This ensures 75% of the index (Duration, Term Premium, Credit Quality) continues working normally as these components only use Yahoo data. The remaining 25% (Yield Curve + Real Rates) uses FRED‚ÜíYahoo fallback hierarchy, never defaulting to stale 50.0 neutral values.

üìà Stocks Fear & Greed Index

The Stocks Index measures sentiment in the equity market using 7 components:

1. Momentum (20%)

RSI + 50-day & 200-day moving averages

S&P 500 (SPY) RSI combined with price position relative to MAs. Weighted 60% RSI, 40% MA position.

2. VIX (20%)

Volatility Index (inverted)

VIX <12 = extreme complacency (greed), VIX >30 = panic (extreme fear).

3. Market Breadth (15%)

Equal-weight vs cap-weight performance

Compares RSP (equal-weight) to SPY. Equal-weight outperforming = broad participation = greed.

4. Sector Rotation (15%)

Tech vs Defensive rotation (QQQ vs XLP)

Measures capital flows between offensive (technology) and defensive (consumer staples) sectors. QQQ (Nasdaq-100) outperforming XLP (Consumer Staples) = risk-on sentiment = greed. XLP outperforming QQQ = defensive rotation = fear. Captures intra-equity risk appetite before broader market moves.

5. Junk Bond Demand (15%)

High-yield vs Treasuries

HYG outperforming TLT = risk-on. TLT outperforming = flight to safety.

6. Safe Haven Demand (10%)

Treasury bond flows (inverted)

TLT rising = fear. TLT falling = risk-on sentiment.

7. Price Strength (5%)

SPY 14-day momentum

Simple 14-day price momentum. Positive = greed, negative = fear.

14-day annualized volatility (inverted)

High volatility (>40%) = extreme fear. Low volatility (<20%) = confidence.

4. Bitcoin Dominance (25%)

BTC vs ETH performance (inverted)

BTC outperforming ETH = capital rotating to safety (fear). ETH outperforming = altcoin season (greed).

14-day price change

Short-term Bitcoin price momentum. Captures immediate market direction.

Market Sentiment Indicator (Risk-On vs Risk-Off)

Visualizes overall market risk appetite by comparing risk assets (Stocks + Crypto) against safe havens (Bonds + Gold). This multi-asset approach provides a comprehensive view of capital rotation and market regime, going beyond simple two-asset comparisons.

Calculation

Risk-On = (Stocks Score + Crypto Score) / 2
Risk-Off = (Bonds Score + Gold Score) / 2
Market Sentiment = Risk-On - Risk-Off
Position = ((Market Sentiment + 100) / 200) √ó 100

This produces a 0-100 position representing the balance between risk appetite and defensive positioning. The calculation uses equal weighting (50/50) within each category to capture "pure sentiment" without bias toward traditional or alternative markets.

Interpretation

  • 0-25 (Extreme Risk-Off): Capital fleeing to safe havens (bonds + gold), markets in distress
  • 25-45 (Risk-Off): Defensive rotation dominant, elevated caution across markets
  • 45-55 (Neutral): Balanced sentiment, no dominant flow between risk-on and risk-off
  • 55-75 (Risk-On): Risk appetite increasing, capital flowing to stocks and crypto
  • 75-100 (Extreme Risk-On): Euphoric risk-taking across assets, potential top warning

Why this approach? Traditional markets (stocks/bonds) and alternative assets (crypto/gold) each provide unique insights into investor behavior. By combining all four indices, we capture a more complete picture of risk sentiment than any single pair of assets could provide.

Frequently Asked Questions

How accurate is the Fear and Greed Index?

Our indices use multi-component quantitative models designed to capture market sentiment shifts. Extreme readings (< 25 or > 75) tend to be more reliable as contrarian signals, while mid-range values reflect genuine market uncertainty. These are analytical tools designed to complement‚Äînot replace‚Äîfundamental analysis and risk management. No sentiment indicator is perfectly predictive, and past patterns may not repeat in future market conditions.

Why does the index sometimes react slowly to major news?

All indices use 14-day moving averages to capture sustained sentiment shifts rather than panic reactions. This means sudden 1-day events take 3-5 days to fully reflect in the scores. This is intentional‚Äîwe track capital rotation and structural trends, not headlines. For day trading signals, other tools may be more appropriate.

Is this better than CNN's Fear and Greed Index?

CNN's index only covers US stocks. We provide four separate indices for Bonds, Gold, Stocks, and Crypto, enabling comprehensive cross-asset sentiment analysis. Our methodology is fully documented and transparent, unlike CNN's proprietary calculations. The Market Sentiment Indicator visualizes risk-on/risk-off flows across all asset classes‚Äîa feature not available elsewhere.

Currently, all data is available via public JSON endpoints (e.g., /data/bonds-fear-greed.json) updated daily at 02:00 UTC. These files contain current scores, component breakdowns, and 365 days of historical data.

Why are there different numbers of components for each index?

Each asset class has unique market dynamics requiring tailored indicators:

  • Bonds (5 components): Bond market sentiment focuses purely on fixed income indicators‚Äîcapital flows (TLT momentum 30%), term premium demand (25%), credit quality spreads (LQD vs TLT, 20%), yield curve shape (15%), and real yields (10%). Zero overlap with equity markets ensures independent bond-specific sentiment measurement. Optimized to measure duration demand and defensive capital rotation.
  • Gold (7 components): Safe haven behavior requires tracking dollar strength, real rates, equity market fear (VIX), ETF flows, momentum, volatility, and relative performance vs stocks. Captures full spectrum of flight-to-safety dynamics.
  • Stocks (7 components): Equity sentiment focuses on breadth, volatility, sector rotation, credit market signals, safe haven demand, and price momentum. Multi-faceted approach captures both technical and fundamental equity market dynamics.
  • Crypto (5 components): Highly momentum-driven market emphasizes trend strength (context 35%), Bitcoin dominance (25%), volatility (15%), price momentum (15%), and technical momentum (RSI 10%). Streamlined for crypto's unique volatility profile.

More components doesn't mean better‚Äîit means more relevant. Each index is optimized for its specific market.
