How This Index is Calculated

The Gold Index measures sentiment TOWARDS gold using 5 components. Like Alternative.me measures crypto sentiment, we measure whether investors are buying or selling gold, regardless of the reason. All scores from 0-100:

1. GLD Price Momentum (30%)

Direct 14-day GLD performance (PRIMARY INDICATOR)

Highest weight component. Measures direct SPDR Gold Trust (GLD) price change over 14 days. GLD +10% = score 100 (extreme greed), GLD -10% = score 0 (extreme fear). This captures actual buying/selling sentiment regardless of macro reasons. Uses a x5 multiplier centered at 50.

2. RSI & Moving Averages (25%)

Mean-reversion and trend signal

Combines 14-day RSI with price position relative to 50-day and 200-day moving averages. Uses proportional distance-based scoring: contribution scales with how far price is above/below each MA, rather than binary on/off signals. RSI provides a +/-25 point contribution centered at 50. Price above MA50 by 10% = strong bullish signal, price below both MAs with RSI < 30 = extreme fear.

3. Dollar Index (20%)

USD strength (inverted correlation)

Gold and the dollar typically move inversely. Weakening dollar = stronger gold demand = higher score. Tracks 14-day change in DXY (US Dollar Index vs basket of currencies) with a x15 multiplier. Dollar down 3.3% = score 100, dollar up 3.3% = score 0. Also factors in DXY position relative to its 30-day moving average.

4. Real Rates (15%)

10-Year TIPS yield (inflation-adjusted)

Gold is a non-yielding asset, so lower real rates = more attractive gold = higher score. Uses FRED API (DFII10 - 10-Year Treasury Inflation-Indexed Security) with Yahoo fallback (TIP ETF). Real rate at -1% = score ~94, at +3% = score ~19. Fundamental driver of gold valuation.

5. VIX (10%)

Safe haven demand (z-score normalized)

Uses a z-score approach: compares current VIX to its 3-month average and standard deviation. VIX significantly above average = market stress = increased safe haven gold demand = higher score. Score = 50 + (z-score x 25), capped 0-100. This contextualizes the VIX level rather than using fixed thresholds, making the signal adaptive to changing volatility regimes.
