How the Sentiment Score is Calculated

The score above measures overall market mood by comparing how investors feel about risk assets (Stocks + Crypto) versus safe havens (Bonds + Gold). When money flows toward safety, the market is fearful. When money flows toward risk, the market is greedy.

Formula

Greed Side = (Stocks Score + Crypto Score) / 2
Fear Side = (Bonds Score + Gold Score) / 2
Market Sentiment = Greed Side - Fear Side
Position = ((Market Sentiment + 100) / 200) × 100

This produces a 0-100 score. Below 50, fear dominates ‚Äî investors prefer safe havens. Above 50, greed dominates ‚Äî investors chase risk. Equal weighting (50/50) within each side avoids bias toward traditional or alternative markets.

Interpretation

  • 0-25 (Extreme Fear): Investors fleeing to safe havens (bonds + gold), markets in distress
  • 25-45 (Fear): Cautious sentiment, investors favoring safety over risk
  • 45-55 (Neutral): No clear direction, balanced between fear and greed
  • 55-75 (Greed): Growing risk appetite, investors favoring stocks and crypto
  • 75-100 (Extreme Greed): Euphoric risk-taking across assets, potential top warning
