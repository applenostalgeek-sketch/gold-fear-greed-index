Our index aggregates 7 key indicators that measure different aspects of gold market sentiment. Each component is scored from 0 (Extreme Fear) to 100 (Extreme Greed).

Component Weight What It Measures
Momentum 25% Price vs moving averages (MA50/MA200) + RSI(14). High momentum = Strong gold demand.

ETF Flows 15% GLD (SPDR Gold Trust) volume and price trends. Increasing flows = Higher demand.
Volatility 15% 14-day volatility vs 30-day average. Lower volatility = More confidence.
Dollar Index 10% DXY 14-day change. Weak dollar = Strong gold (inverse correlation).
VIX 10% Market volatility index. High VIX = Flight to safe havens like gold.
Real Rates 10% 10-Year TIPS yields. Lower real rates = Higher gold appeal (no opportunity cost).

‚ùì Why Our Score Differs from Others

You might notice our index shows different values than other gold sentiment indices (like JM Bullion's). This is intentional and expected. Here's why:

1. Different Methodologies

Other indices may use proprietary formulas, different component weights, or access to non-public data (physical demand, retail surveys, etc.). We focus on publicly verifiable indicators.

2. Macro Context Included

We include macro indicators (VIX, Real Rates, Dollar) that other indices might ignore. This gives a more complete picture but can result in more conservative scores.

3. Balanced Weighting

We distribute weights across 7 indicators rather than heavily weighting 1-2 metrics. This reduces the impact of any single anomalous reading.

4. No Commercial Bias

Unlike indices published by gold dealers, we have no incentive to show artificially high "greed" scores. Our methodology is objective and conservative.
